Canton House - The restaurant serves dim sum for breakfast and lunch on Saturday and Sunday only. People push all the dishes around on carts, and you select what you'd like when they stop by your table. You can also order from a dim sum menu. This is the only dim sum place that I know of near RIT, so if you want some this is the place to go. For those of you who do not know what dim sum is or have never experienced dim sum, you usually go with a group of friends or family and the staff will push around dishes on a cart. You can select what you want from each cart and you pay per dish. The plates are small portions and cost anywhere between $2 to $5 or $6.

Sodam Korean Restaurant - A Korean restaurant in the Genesee Valley Regional Market (large sign on Jefferson Road). It is hard to find, you will have to look for it behind Lori's Natural Foods.

D’Mangu Restaurant - D'Mangu Dominican Restaurant is a Dominican restaurant, in fact the only one, in Henrietta. Many RIT students learn about D’Mangu because they are “visiting chefs” on campus at Brick City, RITZ and in Crossroads. They are usually on campus two or three times a week, but if that isn’t enough for you then definitely go visit the restaurant! It has a beautiful dining area, and the food is delicious.

Osaka Sushi - Osaka Sushi is an all-you-can-eat Japanese restaurant. They are located in Chili Paul Plaza. We highly recommend eating at this restaurant for sushi. Absolutely amazing! The all-you-can-eat is not only for sushi, but also for appetizers, sashimi, fried tempura, dinner entrees, and dessert! Some of the tastiest appetizers include the gyozas, Japanese wontons, spring rolls, avocado salad, seaweed salad, and fried scallops. All kinds of fish are available, including salmon, tuna, white tuna, yellowtail, red snapper, and eel! There are over 20+ different sushi rolls that you can order, some of our favorites are the Volcano Roll, Osaka Roll, and Dragon Roll! Make sure to order some shrimp tempura, sweet potato tempura, teriyaki, fried rice, or udon! There’s too much on the menu to list, you’ll just have to check it out.

Thali of India - Thali of India is an Indian restaurant located in Win-Jeff Plaza. They offer a daily lunch buffet, as well as a dinner buffet on Monday evenings. They are also visiting chefs on campus at Brick City, RITZ, and in Crossroads. They are usually on campus two or three times a week, but if that isn’t enough for you then go and try the lunch buffet or dinner buffet! They have all kinds of curry, rice and side dishes. Also, they constantly refill your table with fresh naan and it’s absolutely amazing.

SEA Restaurant- A Vietnamese restaurant on the corner of Monroe Avenue and Rutgers Street that serves a variety of dishes from Vietnam and Thailand. They feature a lot of traditional Vietnamese and Thai flavors, including mint, cilantro, lime and peanuts. This restaurant has a nice authentic feel when you dine in. Their pho and rice vermicelli dinner entrees taste authentic and delicious. So if the weather is snowy and cold in Rochester, this is a great place to get a huge bowl of nice warm soup for only $8.

Seoul House - Seoul House is a Korean restaurant on West Henrietta Road in Brighton. Most of the tables have built-in grills, for barbecue. Dinner comes with several side dishes including kimchi, radish, tofu, black beans, and steamed rice. Korean BBQ is very popular among Americans and it is a great dinner option for a large group of friends. Order a ton of meat that you can cook in front of you, and it comes with lettuce, rice and a bunch of side dishes. You can wrap the rice and meat inside the lettuce and they also give you this delicious sauce.
